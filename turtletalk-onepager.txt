TURTLETALK
A voice-first AI companion that helps kids ages 5-10 talk through feelings,
and turns those conversations into warm offline moments with their parents.

================================================================================
THE PROBLEM
================================================================================
1 in 5 U.S. children ages 3-17 has a diagnosable mental, emotional, or
behavioral disorder (CDC). Post-pandemic rates of childhood anxiety climbed
roughly 25-30%. Yet access to support is broken:

  - Child therapy wait times in most U.S. cities: 3-9 months
  - Out-of-pocket cost: $100-300 per session
  - Parents ask "How was school?" and get "Fine." — the single most common
    failure mode of parent-child communication in the 5-10 age bracket
  - Every "kids app" on the market is built on the wrong loop: passive
    screen time, flashy graphics, addictive engagement — the exact opposite
    of what a struggling child actually needs

The gap we attack: there is no product today that a parent can hand to their
child in a hard moment that (a) actually listens, (b) is emotionally safe,
and (c) brings the parent back INTO the conversation instead of replacing them.

================================================================================
THE SOLUTION
================================================================================
TurtleTalk is a voice-first companion shaped like Tammy, a sea turtle.

  - Kids TALK to Tammy in natural conversation about anything on their mind
  - Tammy REFLECTS with warm voice replies, gentle reframes, and age-
    appropriate stories — never judgment, never scripts that feel canned
  - Each session generates a "brave mission" (e.g., "tomorrow, say hi to
    someone new") that turns feelings into small real-world action
  - Parents get a weekly digest + one soft prompt at dinner:
    "Ask her about the quiet kid in art class." That's it. No dashboards
    full of transcripts. The goal is bridge-building, not surveillance.

We are not building a therapist. We are building the tiny, patient friend
every kid wishes they had — one that pushes them back toward their parents,
not away.

================================================================================
WHY NOW — TECHNICAL PLAUSIBILITY
================================================================================
TurtleTalk was impossible 18 months ago. Three things just changed:

  1. Low-latency voice AI is finally natural
     Sub-500ms end-to-end response times are now reliably achievable via
     LiveKit Agents, Vapi, and Gemini Live. Kids disengage above ~800ms;
     we are finally below that threshold.

  2. LLM safety tooling matured
     Constitutional AI, structured outputs, response filtering, and
     provider-level safety tiers now make it possible to deploy a
     conversational AI to children with defensible guardrails.

  3. TTS crossed the "warmth" threshold
     ElevenLabs and competitors now produce child-friendly voices that
     carry emotion — not robotic, not uncanny. Trust is a voice problem
     before it is a content problem.

================================================================================
HOW IT WORKS
================================================================================
Frontend:     Next.js + TypeScript, React Three Fiber for 3D Tammy
Voice:        Pluggable pipeline — LiveKit Agents (primary), Vapi, Gemini
              Live — so we A/B-test latency and naturalness without rewrites
LLM:          Claude / GPT / Gemini routed via LangChain with strict
              system prompts + hybrid scripted fallbacks for high-risk topics
Safety:       Three-layer — prompt constraints, live response filtering,
              and an async human review queue for flagged conversations
TTS:          ElevenLabs (child-friendly, expressive, low-latency)
Backend:      Supabase — child auth, mission ledger, weekly reports,
              parent dashboard, row-level privacy
Deploy:       Vercel + serverless functions

The hardest engineering problem is NOT generating good replies — it is
knowing when to STOP generating freely and defer to curated, scripted
content. We treat that boundary as a first-class product surface.

================================================================================
WHAT'S NOVEL — THE MOAT
================================================================================
  1. Hybrid AI + scripted architecture for emotional safety, not pure LLM.
     This is a technical choice most competitors refuse to make because it
     is harder to build — and it is exactly what makes us defensible to
     parents, regulators, and schools.

  2. The "mission" loop converts conversations into offline family action.
     Every interaction ends pointing AWAY from the app. This is the
     opposite of the attention economy and precisely why parents will trust
     us.

  3. Brand-as-character. Tammy is not a skin on a chatbot. The turtle
     shape, voice, and mannerisms are designed to carry emotional transfer
     the way a plush toy does — a research-backed pattern from child
     psychology that pure AI assistants cannot replicate.

  4. Under-served age band. Ages 5-10 sit in a gap: too old for toddler
     apps, too young for teen mental-health tools. Every major player is
     either above or below us.
